Courses


BS in Computer Engineering (5-yr)

The BS Computer Engineering course is designed to give strong foundations in physics, mathematics, electronics, programming methodology, data communication and computer organization to hone students in dealing with the theory and design of algorithms, hardware, software and electronic systems. The program encompasses the study of hardware design, data storage, computer architecture, assembly languages, and design of computers for engineering, information retrieval, and scientific researches. Graduates of the course can choose to enter careers that involve the designs of computer hardware and software systems in various areas such as: computer graphics, computer-aided design, multimedia systems, databases, parallel computation, distributed computation, artificial intelligence, optical computing, large-scale-integration design, and fabrication as computer engineers.

BS in Tourism Management (4-yr)

The BS Tourism Management course provides students with focused skills on tourism in areas besides hospitality. In depth understanding and mastery of skills applied in various sectors that include Tourism Services, Attractions, Events, Seminars & Conferences, Transportation, Travel/Trade/Business and Adventure/Recreation are included in the program. Graduates of the course can choose to enter careers that involve organization and execution of events for various industries requiring travels, seminars/conventions and special events as junior middle manager positions.

BS in Office Administration (4-yr)

The BS in Office Administration is designed for students who desire to enter the business world as office managers or administrative assistants. Emphasis is placed on office procedures, communications, office automation and general office management and coordination skills. Graduates of the course can choose to enter careers that involve managing small to medium scale offices of any business field and serve as support to managerial and supervisory tasks as Administrative support staff.

BS in Information Technology (4-yr)

The BS information Technology course prepares students to become professionals in the digital age imparting comprehensive know-how of the leading information and communication technologies, computer programs and graphics through the application of industry standardized competencies. Graduates of the course can choose to enter careers that involve analysis of design through the use of modern tools to administer computer networks, web pages and other peripherals for any industry utilizing computer technology for information required by the different functional units of a company as I.T. Consultants.

BS in Hotel and Restaurant Management (4-yr)

The BS Hotel and Restaurant Management course provides students with a broad base of management, technical skills and competencies needed to become industry leaders in the competitive hotel and restaurant business environment. Graduates of this degree can choose to enter careers in lodging, restaurant, travel, attractions, meeting and convention, and food service industries as junior and middle managers.

BS in Computer Science (4-yr)

The BS Computer Science course is designed for students seeking an integrated project-based curriculum that focuses on the computer skills highly valued in today’s industry. Projects and coursework are designed to provide strong foundations in technical skills, standards, and fundamentals such as programming, data structures and algorithms, and computer architecture as well as advanced study in operating systems, compilers and artificial intelligence. Graduates of the course can choose to enter careers that involve computer programming, software designing, and computer systems creation in any industry utilizing computer applications in aiding daily operational functions as computer scientists.

BS in Business Administration (4-yr)

The BS in Business Administration Major in Management course provides an empirical study on the theories and principles of modern business management. Students are trained to sharpen essential business skills such as applying quantitative business methods to managerial decision making process, case study analysis of business problems applied in the real world, and project feasibility study preparation. Students gain a general background on business through accounting, financial management, marketing, human resource management, operations management, and organizational studies of various business firms. Graduates of the course can choose to enter business careers in various industries such as banking and finance; accounting, merchandising, manufacturing, and service businesses; or as entrepreneurs that would serve as catalysts of business growth and development.

Electronics Technician (2-yr)

Electronics Technician course offers knowledge and skills development needed to disassemble and assemble, install, troubleshoot, maintain and repair of electronics equipment used by consumers, businesses, and industries. Extensive hands-on and practical laboratory exercises, training in electronics fabrication, electric circuit analysis, design and troubleshooting, digital electronics and microprocessors provide students with exposure to current industry techniques and equipment. Graduates of the course may find careers that involve installation, repair and maintenance of electronics equipment in various industries as electronics technicians.

Computer Technician (2-yr)

The Computer Technician course offers knowledge and skills development in computer troubleshooting based on mastery of hardware problems, principles and application of computer related equipments. Extensive hands-on and practical laboratory exercises provide students with exposure to current industry techniques and equipment. Graduates of the course can choose to enter careers that involve installation, repair and maintenance of computer peripherals in various industries as computer, network and peripheral service technicians.

Computer Secretarial (2-yr)

Computer Secretarial course aims to develop office personnel who will have the working knowledge in basic computer applications and competency in oral & written communication. Graduates of the course can choose to enter careers that involve staff positions aiding departments in various industries as support for managerial positions as secretaries, clerks and staff assistants.

Computer Science (2-yr)

The Computer Science course is designed for students seeking a fast approach through a condensed curriculum that focuses on the computer skills needed in today’s industry. Course works are designed to provide basic foundations in technical skills, standards, and fundamentals such as programming, data structures and algorithms, and computer architecture. Graduates of this program can choose to enter careers in various industries requiring usage of computer technology in the creation of programs mainly for automation and enhancement of technical efficiency in the functional areas of a business through the application of computer programming.
